39. InGel Therapeutics

Vision Restoration via Stem Cells

Sourcing Note: InGel Therapeutics was a President's Innovation Challenge 2023 finalist, affiliated with Harvard Innovation Labs. Use with caution per assignment sourcing rules.

1. Startup Name

InGel Therapeutics

2. Founder Contact

HBS/HMS Team

Harvard Business School / Harvard Medical School

3. Product Overview

InGel Therapeutics leverages stem cell and tissue engineering technology to rescue and restore vision for blind patients. The company is developing regenerative medicine approaches to treat degenerative eye diseases that currently have no cure.

4. Funding Stage

Early Stage — President's Innovation Challenge 2023 finalist.

5. How I Found the Company

Discovered through Harvard President's Innovation Challenge 2023 finalist announcements in the health track.

Strengths

  • Massive unmet need: Millions suffer from vision loss with limited treatment options.
  • Harvard research backing: Access to world-class stem cell and ophthalmology research infrastructure.

Weaknesses

  • Long development timeline: Regenerative medicine therapies require extensive clinical trials and FDA approval.
  • Capital intensive: Biotech development requires significant funding before revenue generation.

7. Five Questions for the Team

  1. What specific eye conditions are you targeting and what is the patient population size?
  2. What stage of preclinical development are you in and what are your FDA pathway plans?
  3. What IP do you have around your stem cell and tissue engineering approach?
  4. How much capital do you estimate needing to reach clinical trials?
  5. Who are the key scientific advisors and what is their track record in ophthalmology?
