Digital Health

35. Omada Health

Virtual Chronic Care Platform

1. Startup Name

Omada Health

2. Founder Contact

Sean Duffy, MD MBA — Co-Founder & CEO

Harvard Medical School MD, Harvard Business School MBA

3. Product Overview

Omada Health is a virtual-first chronic care management platform addressing diabetes, hypertension, prediabetes, weight management, and musculoskeletal conditions. Care teams implement clinically-validated behavior change protocols, combining digital tools, coaching, and connected devices to improve outcomes at scale.

4. Funding Stage

Late Stage — Raised over $500M total from top-tier investors including General Catalyst, Andreessen Horowitz, and Cigna Ventures. Valued at approximately $1B+.

5. How I Found the Company

Discovered as a prominent Harvard HMS/HBS alumni success story in digital health, frequently cited in Rock Health reports and healthcare venture discussions.

Strengths

  • Clinical evidence: Multiple peer-reviewed studies demonstrating outcomes, including CDC-recognized Diabetes Prevention Program.
  • Scale and enterprise relationships: Works with major employers and health plans covering millions of lives.

Weaknesses

  • Late-stage valuation: May be less attractive for early-stage investment thesis given existing scale.
  • Competitive pressure: Livongo (now Teladoc), Virta Health, and others compete aggressively in chronic care space.

7. Five Questions for the Team

  1. What is your current member enrollment and annual retention rate?
  2. How do you differentiate from Livongo/Teladoc's integrated chronic care offerings?
  3. What is your path to profitability and current burn rate?
  4. How are you expanding into new condition categories beyond diabetes?
  5. What are your IPO or exit timeline considerations?
