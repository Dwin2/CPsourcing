5. How I Found the Company

I discovered Inquisio through the Harvard Alumni Entrepreneurs Accelerator 2024 cohort announcement. While GovTech is not strictly Digital Health or Education, the company addresses transparency and civic engagement — which has educational value for democracy. The public records request market is underserved by technology, with most local governments using manual, paper-based processes.

Strengths

  • Clear pain point with inefficient incumbents: Public records requests are a compliance burden for every local government. Manual processing is slow and expensive. A software solution that reduces staff time while improving response speed has quantifiable ROI.
  • Regulatory mandate creates demand: FOIA compliance is legally required, not optional. Governments must respond to requests regardless of budget, creating stable baseline demand for tools that improve efficiency.

Weaknesses

  • Different sector focus: Inquisio is a GovTech company, not in the Digital Health or Education verticals.
  • Government sales cycles are notoriously slow: Procurement in local government involves RFPs, budget cycles, and multi-stakeholder approval. Sales cycles can extend 12-24 months, requiring patience and capital.
