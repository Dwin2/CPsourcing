18. Mind Brain Emotion

EQ Skill-Building Card Games & Mental Health Tools

1. Startup Name

Mind Brain Emotion

2. Founder Contact

Dr. Jenny Woo — Founder & CEO

Harvard Graduate School of Education (M.Ed.); UC Irvine (Ph.D.); UC Berkeley (MBA)

LinkedIn: Search "Jenny Woo Mind Brain Emotion"

Website: mindbrainemotion.com

3. Product Overview

Mind Brain Emotion creates EQ-focused card games that train people of all ages on emotional intelligence skills including relationship building, critical thinking, resilience, and emotion regulation. The product line includes 11 different card games (52 Essential series) sold primarily through Amazon and used in 50+ countries.

4. Funding Stage

Bootstrapped / Profitable — Self-funded with ~$1,000 initial investment in 2018. Generated $1.71 million revenue in 2023 through Amazon sales. No disclosed venture funding. Products used in 50+ countries.

5. How I Found the Company

I discovered Mind Brain Emotion through a CNBC profile on Dr. Jenny Woo's journey from Harvard research to building a $1.7M Amazon business. The company represents an interesting bootstrapped success story in the education/mental health space. The founder's Harvard M.Ed. credential and background as a Montessori school director provide educational credibility.

Strengths

  • Proven profitability without VC: Generating $1.71M revenue in 2023 from a $1,000 initial investment demonstrates exceptional capital efficiency and product-market fit. The business doesn't require venture funding to grow, which is rare in edtech.
  • Multiple credibility signals: Featured by Forbes, CNBC, Fortune, and Parents magazine. The "52 Essential" card series has become a recognized brand in the SEL (social-emotional learning) space, creating defensible brand equity.

Weaknesses

  • Amazon dependency creates platform risk: Primary sales channel is Amazon, which means the business is subject to Amazon's algorithm changes, fee increases, and competitive dynamics. Diversifying distribution is a key risk mitigation priority.
  • Physical product margins and logistics: Card games require manufacturing, inventory management, and shipping — creating complexity and lower margins compared to purely digital products. International expansion adds further logistical challenges.

7. Five Questions for the Team

  1. What is your channel diversification strategy beyond Amazon — are you pursuing direct sales, school district partnerships, or retail expansion?
  2. Have you considered digital versions of the card games (apps, online platforms) that could reduce manufacturing dependency and expand addressable market?
  3. What is your gross margin on physical card products, and how does that trend as you scale?
  4. How do you measure the educational efficacy of the card games — is there research validating EQ skill improvement from using the products?
  5. Are you seeking growth capital to accelerate expansion, or do you prefer to remain bootstrapped and profitable?
