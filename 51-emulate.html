51. Emulate

Organs-on-Chips Technology

1. Startup Name

Emulate

2. Founder Contact

Wyss Institute Team

Wyss Institute for Biologically Inspired Engineering at Harvard

Founded by Don Ingber, MD PhD (Wyss Institute Founding Director) and team

3. Product Overview

Emulate's Organs-on-Chips technology recreates human biology to transform how scientists research diseases and develop drugs. The platform uses microfluidic devices lined with human cells that mimic the physiology of human organs, enabling more accurate predictions of drug efficacy and toxicity than traditional animal testing.

4. Funding Stage

Series E — Raised $225M+ in total funding from investors including Northpond Ventures and others.

5. How I Found the Company

Discovered through Wyss Institute spinout company portfolio and biotechnology innovation tracking.

Strengths

  • Revolutionary technology: Organs-on-Chips can reduce drug development costs and timelines significantly.
  • Scientific credibility: Wyss Institute backing provides unparalleled research validation.

Weaknesses

  • Long sales cycles: Pharma adoption of new methodologies takes years.
  • Regulatory acceptance: FDA and EMA still developing frameworks for organ-chip data.

7. Five Questions for the Team

  1. How many pharmaceutical companies are actively using your platform?
  2. What organs have the highest commercial demand for chip development?
  3. How do you validate that organ-chip results translate to human outcomes?
  4. What is your regulatory strategy for replacing animal testing requirements?
  5. How do you compete with other organ-on-chip companies like Tissuse or CN Bio?
