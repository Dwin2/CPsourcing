3. Product Overview

Sporo Health provides AI-powered clinical workflow tools — including Scribe AI and CoPilots — that automate patient chart reviews, medical note generation, and clinical summarisation. The platform integrates directly with existing Electronic Health Record (EHR) systems, reducing physician documentation burden across primary care, emergency medicine, and nursing facilities.

I began by searching for early-stage digital health startups with Harvard-affiliated founders using public databases and Harvard alumni networks. Sporo Health appeared in the Harvard Innovation Labs' 2024 Year-in-Review milestone list, which highlighted ventures that had achieved significant traction. I cross-referenced the founding team's backgrounds on LinkedIn and confirmed the Harvard SEAS and Harvard Medical School affiliations. The company's focus on AI-driven clinical documentation — a rapidly growing segment of digital health — and its relatively modest funding made it stand out as an under-the-radar opportunity compared to larger players like Nuance/DAX.
