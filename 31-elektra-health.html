31. Elektra Health

Menopause Healthcare Platform

1. Startup Name

Elektra Health

2. Founder Contact

Jannine Versi — Co-Founder & CEO

Harvard Business School MBA '14

Previously at McKinsey and Progyny

3. Product Overview

Elektra Health is a comprehensive menopause healthcare platform offering virtual care, education, and community support. The platform connects women with menopause-trained clinicians for personalized treatment plans, including hormone therapy consultations, and provides evidence-based resources for navigating perimenopause and menopause.

4. Funding Stage

Seed — Raised $3.3M from Seven Seven Six (Alexis Ohanian), Flare Capital Partners, and UPMC Enterprises.

5. How I Found the Company

Discovered through HBS alumni funding news tracking Winter 2024 updates. The company was highlighted among HBS MBA alumni who raised venture capital for healthcare startups.

Strengths

  • Massive underserved market: 1.1 billion women worldwide will be in menopause by 2025, yet most receive inadequate care. FemTech for menopause is rapidly growing.
  • Strong investor backing: Seven Seven Six and Flare Capital bring healthcare expertise and network effects.

Weaknesses

  • Competitive landscape: Faces competition from Midi Health, Gennev, and other menopause-focused startups with more funding.
  • Reimbursement challenges: Many menopause services are not well-covered by insurance, limiting B2C growth.

7. Five Questions for the Team

  1. What is your current patient volume and monthly growth rate?
  2. How do you differentiate from Midi Health and other menopause platforms?
  3. What percentage of revenue comes from B2B (employer) vs B2C channels?
  4. What is your clinician retention rate and how do you train providers?
  5. What are your plans for the next funding round and key milestones?
