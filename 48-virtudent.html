48. Virtudent

Tele-Dentistry Platform

1. Startup Name

Virtudent

2. Founder Contact

Shant Hagopian — Co-Founder

Harvard Law School '15

Former student in HLS Transactional Law Clinic

3. Product Overview

Virtudent is a tele-dentistry startup designed to increase oral health care access for underserved populations. The platform connects patients with dental professionals virtually, enabling screenings, consultations, and care coordination.

4. Funding Stage

Early Stage — Founded during HLS, grew through legal clinic support.

5. How I Found the Company

Discovered through Harvard Law School entrepreneurship program alumni stories and the Transactional Law Clinic case studies.

Strengths

  • Underserved market: 74M Americans lack dental insurance, and access is a major barrier.
  • Legal/regulatory expertise: Founder's HLS background helps navigate complex teledentistry regulations.

Weaknesses

  • Limitations of virtual dental care: Many procedures require in-person visits.
  • State-by-state regulations: Teledentistry rules vary significantly across states.

7. Five Questions for the Team

  1. What services can you provide virtually vs. requiring referral to in-person care?
  2. How do you partner with dental practices for in-person follow-up?
  3. What is your revenue model — B2C subscription, employer benefit, or Medicaid?
  4. How many states are you currently licensed to operate in?
  5. What clinical outcomes data do you have from your patient population?
