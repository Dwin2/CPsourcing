88. Root Robotics

Educational Coding Robot

1. Startup Name

Root Robotics (Acquired by iRobot 2019)

2. Founder Contact

Wyss Institute Team

Wyss Institute for Biologically Inspired Engineering at Harvard

Spun out of Wyss robotics research

3. Product Overview

Root Robotics created an educational coding robot for K-12 students. The robot and accompanying curriculum teach coding through hands-on play, with a graphical programming interface that scales from early elementary through high school computer science.

4. Funding Stage

Acquired — Acquired by iRobot in 2019 for education market expansion.

5. How I Found the Company

Discovered through Wyss Institute spinout company portfolio and education robotics coverage.

Strengths

  • Successful exit: iRobot acquisition validates the technology and market.
  • Wyss pedigree: World-class robotics research foundation.

Weaknesses

  • Already acquired: Not available for direct investment.
  • Historical case study: Useful for learning but not current opportunity.
