85. Muso

Community Health Worker Model

1. Startup Name

Muso

2. Founder Contact

Ari Johnson, MD

Harvard Medical School / Harvard Kennedy School

Physician and global health innovator

3. Product Overview

Muso's community health worker model eliminates delays to healthcare in Mali. The organization deploys proactive community health workers who conduct door-to-door visits, identifying sick patients and providing immediate treatment or referral, dramatically reducing child mortality.

4. Funding Stage

Growth Stage — Established nonprofit with proven mortality reduction.

5. How I Found the Company

Discovered through Harvard SICI social enterprise network and global health coverage.

Strengths

  • Proven outcomes: Demonstrated 10x reduction in child mortality in program areas.
  • Scalable model: Community health worker approach can be replicated.

Weaknesses

  • Grant dependent: Operating in low-income settings requires philanthropic funding.
  • Context specific: Model developed for Mali may need adaptation elsewhere.

7. Five Questions for the Team

  1. How many community health workers do you currently employ?
  2. What specific mortality reduction have you achieved?
  3. How do you train and retain community health workers?
  4. Are you expanding beyond Mali to other countries?
  5. How do you work with government health systems?
