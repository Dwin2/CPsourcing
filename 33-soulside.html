33. Soulside

Postpartum Mental Health Platform

1. Startup Name

Soulside

2. Founder Contact

Surabhi Bhandari — Co-Founder

Harvard Business School MBA '17

3. Product Overview

Soulside is a postpartum mental health company providing support for new mothers. The platform addresses the critical gap in maternal mental health care, offering resources, therapy connections, and community support during the vulnerable postpartum period when 1 in 5 women experience mood disorders.

4. Funding Stage

Seed — Raised seed funding (amount undisclosed) from undisclosed investors.

5. How I Found the Company

Discovered through HBS alumni funding news and women's health startup tracking in 2024.

Strengths

  • Urgent unmet need: Maternal mental health is severely underfunded despite affecting millions of women annually.
  • Employer benefit potential: Companies increasingly recognize postpartum support as critical for retention of working mothers.

Weaknesses

  • Narrow focus: Postpartum-specific positioning may limit total addressable market compared to broader mental health platforms.
  • Insurance coverage gaps: Mental health reimbursement remains challenging, especially for digital-first solutions.

7. Five Questions for the Team

  1. How do you acquire users — through OB-GYN partnerships, employers, or direct-to-consumer?
  2. What clinical outcomes data do you have from your patient population?
  3. How do you differentiate from general mental health platforms like Talkspace or BetterHelp?
  4. What is your care model — licensed therapists, peer support, or hybrid?
  5. What expansion plans do you have beyond postpartum into broader maternal health?
