40. Kiikter

Childhood Nutrition & Anemia Prevention

Sourcing Note: Kiikter was a President's Innovation Challenge 2023 finalist, affiliated with Harvard Innovation Labs. Use with caution per assignment sourcing rules.

1. Startup Name

Kiikter

2. Founder Contact

HSPH Team

Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health

3. Product Overview

Kiikter develops low-cost fortified snacks to improve children's nutrition and reduce anemia in developing regions. The snacks are fortified with key micronutrients including iron, targeting the 40% of children under 5 in low-income countries who suffer from anemia.

4. Funding Stage

Early Stage — President's Innovation Challenge 2023 finalist.

5. How I Found the Company

Discovered through Harvard President's Innovation Challenge 2023 finalist announcements, showcasing HSPH global health innovation.

Strengths

  • Massive global health impact: Childhood anemia affects hundreds of millions and has lifelong consequences.
  • Public health expertise: HSPH founders bring rigorous nutrition science and global health experience.

Weaknesses

  • Distribution challenges: Reaching children in remote, low-income areas is logistically complex.
  • Price sensitivity: Target market has limited ability to pay, requiring donor or government funding.

7. Five Questions for the Team

  1. What specific micronutrient formulation do you use and what clinical evidence supports it?
  2. What countries are you targeting for initial launch and what are the regulatory requirements?
  3. How do you plan to distribute — through schools, clinics, retail, or NGO partnerships?
  4. What is your cost per child reached and how does it compare to other anemia interventions?
  5. Are you structured as a for-profit social enterprise or nonprofit, and what is your funding strategy?
