52. Gameto

IVF and Fertility Innovation

1. Startup Name

Gameto

2. Founder Contact

Wyss Institute Team

Wyss Institute for Biologically Inspired Engineering at Harvard

Technology developed through Wyss research programs

3. Product Overview

Gameto is making IVF safer, cheaper, and more accessible through ovarian organoid technology. The company's platform uses lab-grown ovarian support cells to improve egg maturation outside the body, potentially reducing the hormone burden on patients and improving IVF success rates.

4. Funding Stage

Series B — Raised $40M+ from prominent fertility and biotech investors.

5. How I Found the Company

Discovered through Wyss Institute spinout company portfolio and women's health technology coverage.

Strengths

  • Massive market: IVF market is $25B+ globally with growing demand.
  • Patient-friendly: Reducing hormone load addresses major patient pain point.

Weaknesses

  • Clinical validation: Must prove efficacy in human trials before commercialization.
  • Regulatory complexity: Novel cell-based therapies face extended approval timelines.

7. Five Questions for the Team

  1. What is the current stage of your clinical trials?
  2. How much can you reduce hormone dosing while maintaining success rates?
  3. What is your go-to-market strategy — partner with clinics or direct sales?
  4. How do you compare to other IVF innovation companies like Kindbody?
  5. What is the expected cost reduction for patients using your technology?
