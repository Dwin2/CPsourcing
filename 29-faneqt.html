29. FanEQT

Sports Asset Monetization & Stadium Fractional Ownership

1. Startup Name

FanEQT

2. Founder Contact

Usman Shuja — Founder

Harvard Kennedy School

HAE Accelerator 2024 cohort

3. Product Overview

FanEQT creates a marketplace for sports asset monetization, including stadium fractional ownership. The platform enables fans to own shares in sports infrastructure and participate in the economics of their favorite teams and venues.

4. Funding Stage

Pre-Seed — Part of Harvard Alumni Entrepreneurs Accelerator 2024 cohort (equity-free). Harvard Kennedy School affiliated. Exact funding not publicly disclosed.

5. How I Found the Company

I discovered FanEQT through the Harvard Alumni Entrepreneurs Accelerator 2024 cohort announcement. While Sports Tech/Fintech is not strictly Digital Health or Education, the company represents an interesting application of fractional ownership models to sports — a space seeing increased attention from investors as traditional ownership structures evolve.

Strengths

  • Novel approach to fan engagement: Fractional ownership gives fans a tangible stake in their teams beyond merchandise and tickets, potentially creating deeper loyalty and new revenue streams for sports organizations.
  • Growing interest in alternative assets: Platforms like Rally, Masterworks, and Otis have proven demand for fractional ownership of collectibles and art. Sports assets could be a natural extension of this trend.

Weaknesses

  • Different sector focus: FanEQT is a Sports Tech/Fintech company, not in the Digital Health or Education verticals.
  • Complex regulatory landscape: Fractional ownership of sports assets may involve securities regulations, which vary by jurisdiction and can be expensive to navigate. Compliance costs could be significant for an early-stage company.

7. Five Questions for the Team

  1. What is the legal/regulatory structure for fractional stadium ownership — are these securities offerings, revenue-sharing agreements, or another model?
  2. What sports organizations or venues have you partnered with or are in discussions with?
  3. What returns or benefits do fractional owners receive — dividends, perks, voting rights, or appreciation potential?
  4. How do you acquire customers — sports team marketing partnerships, direct-to-consumer, or social media?
  5. What is your minimum investment size, and who is your target customer demographic?
