3. Product Overview

Indian School of Democracy is a non-partisan organization that nurtures principled political leaders through programs like "The Good Politician" and "She Represents." The school trains aspiring grassroots leaders (ages 25-40) with concrete political skills, moral courage, and civic imagination to run for public office.

I discovered Indian School of Democracy through the Harvard Kennedy School Mittal South Asia Institute, which profiled Hemakshi Meghani's work on political leadership training. Strengths

  • Unique positioning in massive market: India has 1.4B people and the world's largest democracy, yet there is no established institution training ethical political leaders. ISD is creating a new category with first-mover advantage in an enormous addressable population.
  • Prestigious validation: Echoing Green Fellowship (highly competitive global social entrepreneurship program) and World Bank Graduate Scholar background provide credibility with funders and attract high-quality applicants to the programs.

Weaknesses

  • Long time to measurable impact: Training political leaders today may not result in elected officials for years or decades. Demonstrating impact to funders requires patience and long-term tracking of alumni career progression.
  • Political sensitivity: Operating a "political training school" in India requires careful navigation of partisan dynamics. Maintaining genuine non-partisanship while training people for inherently political roles is challenging.
