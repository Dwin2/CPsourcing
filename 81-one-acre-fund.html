81. One Acre Fund

Smallholder Farmer Training

1. Startup Name

One Acre Fund

2. Founder Contact

Andrew Youn

Harvard Kennedy School

One of the largest social enterprises in Africa

3. Product Overview

One Acre Fund provides agricultural training and financing for smallholder farmers in Africa. The organization supplies seeds, fertilizer, training, and market access to help small-scale farmers significantly increase their crop yields and income.

4. Funding Stage

Growth Stage — Serving 1M+ farmers across Africa with hybrid funding model.

5. How I Found the Company

Discovered through Harvard SICI social enterprise network and global development coverage.

Strengths

  • Proven impact: Average 50%+ yield increases for participating farmers.
  • Scale: 1M+ farmers served demonstrates repeatable model.

Weaknesses

  • Subsidy dependent: Requires philanthropic support to serve poorest farmers.
  • Climate vulnerability: Farmer outcomes affected by weather and climate change.

7. Five Questions for the Team

  1. What is the average income increase for farmers in your program?
  2. How do you measure and ensure training quality at scale?
  3. What countries are you operating in and what is expansion plan?
  4. How do you address climate change impacts on farmer outcomes?
  5. What is your path to financial sustainability vs. grant funding?
