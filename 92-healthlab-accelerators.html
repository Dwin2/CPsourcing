92. HealthLab Accelerators

Student Health Startup Program

1. Startup Name

HealthLab Accelerators

2. Founder Contact

Harvard T.H. Chan SPH / Harvard College

Collaboration between Chan School and undergraduates

Part of Lemann Program support

3. Product Overview

HealthLab Accelerators is a program helping students launch health startups with $20K prizes. The accelerator provides mentorship, resources, and funding to student teams developing innovative solutions to health challenges.

4. Funding Stage

Accelerator Program — Producing pipeline of health startups from Harvard students.

5. How I Found the Company

Discovered through Harvard Chan School innovation programs and Lemann Program coverage.

Strengths

  • Talent pipeline: Access to Harvard health and business students.
  • Structured support: Mentorship and funding accelerates development.

Weaknesses

  • Program vs. company: Not a direct investment opportunity.
  • Variable quality: Student ventures have higher failure rates.

7. Five Questions for the Team

  1. How many student ventures have gone through the program?
  2. What percentage of teams continue after graduation?
  3. What successful companies have emerged from HealthLab?
  4. How do you connect students with healthcare industry mentors?
  5. Are there opportunities to invest in HealthLab portfolio companies?
