38. Patient First.AI

Digital Health Card Platform

Sourcing Note: Patient First.AI won the President's Innovation Challenge 2023, which is affiliated with Harvard Innovation Labs. Use with caution per assignment sourcing rules.

1. Startup Name

Patient First.AI

2. Founder Contact

Fiza Shaukat, MPH — Co-Founder

Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health '22

Farooq Shaukat — Co-Founder

3. Product Overview

Patient First.AI creates a digital health card that addresses all of a patient's healthcare needs in one integrated platform. The solution aims to simplify healthcare navigation by consolidating health information, appointments, records, and care coordination into a single accessible interface.

4. Funding Stage

Pre-Seed — Won $75,000 first-place prize at Harvard President's Innovation Challenge 2023.

5. How I Found the Company

Discovered through Harvard President's Innovation Challenge 2023 winners announcement featuring Harvard Chan School founders.

Strengths

  • Patient-centered approach: Focus on patient experience aligns with value-based care trends.
  • Public health expertise: Founders' HSPH background brings population health perspective.

Weaknesses

  • Crowded space: Many patient engagement and health records apps compete for consumer attention.
  • Integration challenges: Connecting to multiple health systems requires significant technical and business development effort.

7. Five Questions for the Team

  1. What specific features differentiate your digital health card from Apple Health, Epic MyChart, or other patient portals?
  2. How do you handle data integration across different health systems?
  3. What is your go-to-market strategy — direct to consumers, through employers, or via health systems?
  4. How do you ensure data security and HIPAA compliance?
  5. What traction have you achieved since the PIC win and what are your next milestones?
