66. Edstation

Startup School for Preteens

1. Startup Name

Edstation

2. Founder Contact

Nathalia Kelday

Harvard Graduate School of Education '24

Lemann Fellow focused on entrepreneurship education

3. Product Overview

Edstation is a hands-on startup school for preteens solving global challenges. The program teaches entrepreneurship, design thinking, and problem-solving skills to young people aged 8-14 through project-based learning where students build real ventures.

4. Funding Stage

Pre-Seed — 230+ graduates from program, building scalable curriculum.

5. How I Found the Company

Discovered through HGSE alumni network and Lemann Fellow program.

Strengths

  • Growing market: Parents increasingly interested in entrepreneurship education for kids.
  • Proven curriculum: 230+ graduates demonstrate repeatable program model.

Weaknesses

  • Scaling challenge: Experiential programs harder to scale than content platforms.
  • Premium pricing: Entrepreneurship camps typically serve affluent families.
