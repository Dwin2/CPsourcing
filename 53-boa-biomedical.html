53. BOA Biomedical

Pathogen Capture Technology

1. Startup Name

BOA Biomedical

2. Founder Contact

Wyss Institute Team

Wyss Institute for Biologically Inspired Engineering at Harvard

Spun out of Wyss Institute research programs

3. Product Overview

BOA Biomedical develops pathogen-capture technology that removes bacteria, viruses, and toxins directly from the bloodstream. The technology uses engineered binding proteins to capture pathogens in a blood filtration device, potentially treating sepsis and other life-threatening infections.

4. Funding Stage

Early Stage — Developing technology through Wyss Institute support and early venture funding.

5. How I Found the Company

Discovered through Wyss Institute spinout company portfolio and sepsis treatment innovation coverage.

Strengths

  • Urgent clinical need: Sepsis kills 270,000+ Americans annually with limited treatment options.
  • Novel mechanism: Physical removal of pathogens complements antibiotic therapy.

Weaknesses

  • Complex device: Blood filtration devices require intensive clinical validation.
  • ICU adoption: Critical care settings have high barriers to new technology adoption.

7. Five Questions for the Team

  1. What pathogens can your device capture and at what efficiency?
  2. Where are you in the clinical trial process?
  3. How do you integrate with existing dialysis and blood filtration infrastructure?
  4. What is the cost per treatment and how does it compare to standard sepsis care?
  5. Are you pursuing FDA breakthrough device designation?
