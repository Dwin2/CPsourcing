54. NuProbe

Non-Invasive Cancer Diagnostics

1. Startup Name

NuProbe

2. Founder Contact

Wyss Institute Team

Wyss Institute for Biologically Inspired Engineering at Harvard

Founded on DNA toehold probe technology developed at Wyss

3. Product Overview

NuProbe develops DNA toehold probe systems for non-invasive cancer diagnostics. The technology enables highly sensitive detection of circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) in blood samples, allowing earlier cancer detection and monitoring of treatment response without invasive biopsies.

4. Funding Stage

Series A — Raised venture funding to advance product development and clinical validation.

5. How I Found the Company

Discovered through Wyss Institute spinout company portfolio and liquid biopsy technology coverage.

Strengths

  • Growing liquid biopsy market: Non-invasive cancer testing is projected to exceed $10B by 2030.
  • Technical differentiation: Toehold probe technology offers unique sensitivity advantages.

Weaknesses

  • Competitive landscape: Grail, Guardant, and Foundation Medicine dominate liquid biopsy.
  • Reimbursement challenges: Payer coverage for liquid biopsy still inconsistent.

7. Five Questions for the Team

  1. What cancer types does your platform detect most effectively?
  2. How does your sensitivity compare to existing liquid biopsy platforms?
  3. What is your clinical validation strategy and timeline?
  4. Are you targeting screening, diagnosis, or treatment monitoring?
  5. How do you plan to achieve Medicare and commercial payer coverage?
