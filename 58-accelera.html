58. Accelera

Neurological Wearable Devices

1. Startup Name

Accelera

2. Founder Contact

Wyss Institute Team

Wyss Institute for Biologically Inspired Engineering at Harvard

Developing soft robotics for neurological conditions

3. Product Overview

Accelera develops wearable devices supporting neurological function in cerebral palsy patients. Using Wyss Institute's soft robotics and exosuit technology, the devices provide assistive movement support to improve mobility and quality of life for patients with movement disorders.

4. Funding Stage

Early Stage — Developing technology through Wyss Institute support and grant funding.

5. How I Found the Company

Discovered through Wyss Institute spinout company portfolio and assistive technology coverage.

Strengths

  • Unmet need: Limited assistive technology options exist for cerebral palsy patients.
  • Wyss soft robotics: World-leading research in wearable assistive devices.

Weaknesses

  • Small market: Cerebral palsy affects ~500K Americans, limiting market size.
  • Insurance coverage: Durable medical equipment reimbursement is challenging.

7. Five Questions for the Team

  1. What specific movements or functions does your device assist?
  2. What clinical trial data do you have on functional improvement?
  3. How do you customize devices for different severity levels?
  4. What is your pricing strategy and reimbursement pathway?
  5. Are you expanding to other neurological conditions like stroke or MS?
