3. Product Overview

Qalam Health Solutions develops innovative intraoperative cancer-detection devices that enable surgeons to identify bone cancer cells in tissue margins during surgery. The goal is to reduce cancer recurrence, minimise the need for re-operation, and optimise oncological outcomes through real-time surgical guidance.

Strengths

  • Unmet clinical need: Positive surgical margins (incomplete tumour removal) in bone cancer surgeries lead to high recurrence rates and costly re-operations. A real-time, intraoperative detection tool addresses a clear gap where no established standard-of-care exists, giving Qalam first-mover advantage in a niche but critical clinical application.
  • Non-dilutive funding strategy: By leveraging Canadian government grants (Quebec, INOVAIT) and accelerator programmes, the founder has extended runway without heavy equity dilution — preserving upside for future investors while de-risking the technology through grant-funded validation work.
Ask AI to verify or expand

Weaknesses

  • Very early stage with minimal capital: With only ~$7K in disclosed equity funding, the company is extremely early. Moving from concept to a validated medical device requires significant capital for prototyping, clinical validation, and regulatory submissions — likely $2-5 M before any revenue.
  • Narrow initial market: Bone cancer surgery is a relatively small surgical subspecialty. While the clinical need is acute, the total addressable market may be limited compared to broader oncology diagnostics. The company will need a clear expansion strategy into adjacent surgical applications to attract growth-stage investors.

  1. What is the current state of your prototype — has it been tested ex-vivo, in animal models, or in any preliminary human studies?

  4. Beyond bone cancer margins, what are the next surgical applications you envision for the platform, and how large are those markets?
