83. SIRUM

Medication Redistribution

1. Startup Name

SIRUM (Supporting Initiatives to Redistribute Unused Medicine)

2. Founder Contact

Kiah Williams

Harvard Kennedy School

Social entrepreneur focused on healthcare access

3. Product Overview

SIRUM redistributes unused medication to people in need. The nonprofit platform connects facilities with surplus unexpired medications to safety-net pharmacies and clinics serving uninsured and underinsured patients, reducing waste while improving medication access.

4. Funding Stage

Growth Stage — Established nonprofit operating in multiple states.

5. How I Found the Company

Discovered through Harvard SICI social enterprise network and healthcare access coverage.

Strengths

  • Win-win model: Reduces pharmaceutical waste while helping patients.
  • Regulatory momentum: More states passing drug donation laws.

Weaknesses

  • State-by-state: Drug donation laws vary, limiting national scale.
  • Nonprofit funding: Dependent on grants and donations.

7. Five Questions for the Team

  1. How much medication have you redistributed to date?

  3. How do you ensure medication safety and chain of custody?
  4. What types of medications are most commonly donated?
  5. How do you advocate for expanded drug donation laws?
