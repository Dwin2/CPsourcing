3. Product Overview

SeaCycle transforms seafood industry waste into valuable biopolymers, creating sustainable alternatives to petroleum-based plastics. The company extracts chitin and chitosan from shrimp shells, crab exoskeletons, and other seafood byproducts that would otherwise be discarded. These biopolymers have applications in packaging, agriculture, cosmetics, and biomedical products, offering a circular economy solution that addresses both waste management and plastic pollution challenges.

Strengths

  • Dual value proposition addressing waste and plastics: SeaCycle solves two problems simultaneously — diverting millions of tons of seafood waste from landfills while producing sustainable alternatives to petroleum-based plastics. This circular economy model is increasingly attractive to investors and corporate customers.
  • Growing market for sustainable materials: The global biopolymer market is projected to exceed $15 billion by 2027, driven by regulations limiting single-use plastics and consumer demand for sustainable products. Major CPG companies are actively seeking bio-based packaging alternatives.

Weaknesses
