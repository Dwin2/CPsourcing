14. Koroid

AI-Powered Healthcare Workforce Management

1. Startup Name

Koroid

2. Founder Contact

Carl Gustaf Axelsson, MD — CEO

Harvard Business School; Harvard Medical School

Website: koroid.com

3. Product Overview

Koroid is an AI-powered platform for data-driven workforce and resource management, optimization, and logistics in the healthcare industry. The platform uses predictive analytics to forecast patient demand and optimize staffing levels, reducing labor costs without compromising patient care.

4. Funding Stage

Early Stage — Has completed a pilot deployment with Mass General Brigham, one of the most respected academic health systems in the US. Part of Launch Lab X GEO 2023-2024 cohort. Featured in Harvard Business School case study on scaling decisions. Exact funding not publicly disclosed.

5. How I Found the Company

I discovered Koroid through a Harvard Business School case study examining the company's scaling decisions after a successful Mass General Brigham pilot. The founder's dual Harvard credentials (HBS and HMS) and the MGH validation provided strong signals of both business and clinical credibility. Healthcare workforce management is a $2B+ market, and labor costs represent 50-60% of hospital operating expenses — creating significant ROI potential for optimization solutions.

Strengths

  • Elite clinical validation: A successful pilot and deployment at Mass General Brigham — consistently ranked among America's top hospitals — provides exceptional reference value. This credential can open doors to other academic medical centers and large health systems.
  • Large, quantifiable ROI: Labor is 50-60% of hospital operating expenses. If Koroid can demonstrate even 3-5% efficiency gains through predictive staffing, the dollar savings for a large health system could be tens of millions annually — creating a compelling business case.

Weaknesses

  • Complex enterprise sales cycle: Health system workforce decisions involve nursing leadership, HR, finance, IT, and union considerations. The multi-stakeholder sales cycle can extend 12-24 months, requiring significant capital and sales expertise to navigate.
  • Sensitive labor implications: Any workforce optimization tool faces scrutiny about whether "efficiency" means fewer staff or harder working conditions. Koroid must carefully position as enhancing care quality and staff satisfaction, not just cutting headcount.

7. Five Questions for the Team

  1. What were the quantified results from the Mass General Brigham pilot — labor cost savings, staffing prediction accuracy, or patient outcome improvements?
  2. What is your current pipeline and sales cycle length with other health systems?
  3. How does Koroid integrate with existing hospital scheduling systems and EHRs?
  4. How do you address concerns from nursing staff and unions about AI-driven workforce optimization?
  5. What is your fundraising plan — are you pursuing healthcare-focused VCs, and what milestones are you targeting before Series A?
