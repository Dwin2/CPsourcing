  • Large, quantifiable ROI: Labor is 50-60% of hospital operating expenses. If Koroid can demonstrate even 3-5% efficiency gains through predictive staffing, the dollar savings for a large health system could be tens of millions annually — creating a compelling business case.

Weaknesses

  • Complex enterprise sales cycle: Health system workforce decisions involve nursing leadership, HR, finance, IT, and union considerations. The multi-stakeholder sales cycle can extend 12-24 months, requiring significant capital and sales expertise to navigate.
  • Sensitive labor implications: Any workforce optimization tool faces scrutiny about whether "efficiency" means fewer staff or harder working conditions. Koroid must carefully position as enhancing care quality and staff satisfaction, not just cutting headcount.
