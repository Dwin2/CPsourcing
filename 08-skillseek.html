3. Product Overview

SkillSeek operates workforce-development bootcamps that train individuals for careers as HVAC technicians in the clean-energy industry. The programme combines a learner-centred curriculum with applied, on-the-job skills taught by experienced technicians — addressing the critical shortage of trained HVAC professionals as the US transitions to heat pumps and renewable energy systems.

Strengths

  • Exceptional pilot metrics: The first cohort achieved an 82% completion rate, 90% job-match rate, and 100% Net Promoter Score — metrics that would be impressive for any education programme. These results de-risk the model and provide a strong foundation for fundraising and scaling.
  • Massive structural tailwinds: The US faces a shortage of 110,000+ HVAC technicians, exacerbated by the IRA's push toward heat pump adoption. Over 80% of current HVAC positions are filled through personal connections, creating a huge equity gap that SkillSeek is uniquely positioned to address.
Ask AI to verify or expand

Weaknesses

  • Scaling challenges for in-person training: Unlike software-based edtech that scales globally with marginal cost near zero, in-person bootcamps require local facilities, instructors, and employer partnerships in each new market. Geographic expansion is inherently slower and more capital-intensive.
  • Revenue model uncertainty: Workforce bootcamps can charge students (ISAs, upfront tuition), charge employers (recruitment fees), or both. The optimal pricing model for a population that includes "underserved communities" is unclear, and may conflict with the social mission if tuition is too high.

7. Five Questions for the Team

  1. What is your revenue model — do students pay tuition (upfront or through ISAs), do employers pay placement fees, or is it a hybrid?
  2. How many employer partners are committed to hiring your graduates, and what is the average starting salary for placed technicians?
  3. What is your plan for geographic expansion — will you open additional locations, partner with existing trade schools, or develop a hybrid online/in-person model?
  4. How do you differentiate from existing HVAC training programmes at community colleges and trade schools, which already have established employer relationships?
  5. With the Inflation Reduction Act creating massive demand for heat pump installation, how are you positioning SkillSeek to capture the specific heat-pump training opportunity vs. general HVAC?
