4. InConfidence

Smart Patch — At-Home Urinary Incontinence Treatment

Sourcing Note: InConfidence participated in Launch Lab X GEO (Harvard Innovation Labs). Consider framing discovery through the NSW Health Medical Devices Fund or ROHTO partnership announcements instead.

1. Startup Name

InConfidence (operated through Australis Scientific Pty Ltd)

2. Founder Contact

Nicky Agahari — CEO & Founder

Harvard Innovation Labs Fellow; based in Australia

Website: inconfidence.io

LinkedIn: search "Nicky Agahari InConfidence"

3. Product Overview

InConfidence is developing a band-aid-like smart patch that delivers at-home posterior tibial nerve stimulation (PTNS) to treat urinary incontinence and overactive bladder. The patch stimulates the tibial nerve in the ankle, correcting the neural signal between bladder and brain — replacing costly, recurring in-clinic PTNS sessions.

4. Funding Stage

Pre-Seed — Received $750,000 from the NSW Health Medical Devices Fund (Australia) for clinical trials. Also received investment from ROHTO Pharmaceutical (Japan, October 2023). Part of Launch Lab X GEO 2023-2024 cohort.

5. How I Found the Company

I found InConfidence through an article on the NSW Health Medical Research website about an innovative medical device being developed for incontinence. I then discovered the Harvard connection through the founder's fellowship at the Harvard Innovation Labs and his participation in the Launch Lab X GEO programme. The personal origin story — Agahari built the product after discovering his grandfather was one of 200 M+ people affected by urinary incontinence — adds authenticity to the venture. The clinical-trial stage and pharma partnership with ROHTO suggest meaningful technical validation.

Strengths

  • Massive addressable market: Over 200 million people worldwide suffer from urinary incontinence, yet the condition is vastly under-treated due to stigma and inconvenient clinical options. An at-home, discreet wearable that replaces weekly clinic visits could unlock enormous latent demand.
  • Strategic pharma validation: The investment from ROHTO Pharmaceutical — a major Japanese healthcare company — provides not just capital but potential distribution channels across Asia-Pacific, one of the fastest-growing medtech markets.

Weaknesses

  • Lengthy regulatory pathway: As a medical device delivering nerve stimulation, InConfidence will need regulatory clearance in every target market (TGA in Australia, FDA in the US, CE marking in Europe). This is a multi-year, capital-intensive process that introduces significant timeline risk.
  • Geographic fragmentation: The company is based in Australia, received a Harvard fellowship in the US, and has a Japanese pharma partner. Managing operations, clinical trials, and regulatory submissions across three continents with limited resources is challenging for a pre-seed company.

7. Five Questions for the Team

  1. What are the results from your initial clinical trial funded by the NSW Medical Devices Fund, and when do you expect to publish?
  2. What is your regulatory strategy — are you pursuing TGA approval in Australia first as a stepping stone, or going directly for FDA 510(k) clearance?
  3. How does the ROHTO partnership work in practice — is it a distribution agreement, a co-development arrangement, or a pure financial investment?
  4. What is the target price point for the smart patch, and how does that compare to the cost of in-clinic PTNS sessions (typically $200-400/session, 12 sessions needed)?
  5. How do you plan to address the stigma barrier in marketing — will you go DTC to consumers or work through urologists and primary care physicians?
