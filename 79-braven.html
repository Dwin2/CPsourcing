79. Braven

First-Gen Career Readiness

1. Startup Name

Braven

2. Founder Contact

Aimee Eubanks Davis

Harvard Kennedy School

Former Teach for America executive and education leader

3. Product Overview

Braven is a career readiness program for first-generation college students. The organization partners with universities to deliver a for-credit course that helps students develop professional skills, build networks, and secure strong jobs after graduation.

4. Funding Stage

Growth Stage — Established nonprofit with significant philanthropic support.

5. How I Found the Company

Discovered through Harvard SICI social enterprise network and education innovation coverage.

Strengths

  • Proven model: Demonstrated outcomes with thousands of students at partner universities.
  • Social capital focus: Addresses network gap that perpetuates inequality.

Weaknesses

  • Nonprofit reliance: Dependent on philanthropic funding for growth.
  • University partnerships: Scaling requires individual partnerships with each school.

7. Five Questions for the Team

  1. How many students have completed your program?
  2. What employment outcomes do your graduates achieve vs. peers?
  3. How many university partners do you currently have?
  4. What is your cost per student and funding model?
  5. How do you measure long-term career trajectory impact?
