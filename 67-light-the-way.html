67. Light the Way

Brazilian Immigrant Education

1. Startup Name

Light the Way

2. Founder Contact

Juliana Kagami & Luciana Schmidt

Harvard Graduate School of Education '23

HGSE Education Entrepreneurship Fellowship recipients

3. Product Overview

Light the Way empowers Brazilian immigrants through education and community support. The organization provides English language learning, career development, and community building programs to help Brazilian immigrants in the US succeed in their new home.

4. Funding Stage

Pre-Seed — HGSE Education Entrepreneurship Fellowship award recipient.

5. How I Found the Company

Discovered through HGSE Education Entrepreneurship Fellowship program listings.

Strengths

  • Underserved community: Brazilian immigrants are a large, growing population with few targeted resources.
  • Founder connection: Founders have personal understanding of immigrant experience.

Weaknesses

  • Niche focus: Single ethnic community limits total addressable market.
  • Nonprofit model: Grant funding can be inconsistent and limiting.

7. Five Questions for the Team

  1. How many Brazilian immigrants have you served to date?
  2. What specific programs do you offer — ESL, job placement, community events?
  3. What outcomes do you measure for participants?
  4. How do you plan to scale beyond your current geography?
  5. Are you exploring partnerships with employers seeking bilingual workers?
