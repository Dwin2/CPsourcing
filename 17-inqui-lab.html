17. Inqui-Lab Foundation

Problem-Solving & Innovation Education for First-Generation Learners in India

1. Startup Name

Inqui-Lab Foundation

2. Founder Contact

Eshwar Bandi — Co-Founder

Harvard Graduate School of Education '23 (M.Ed.)

Also a 2022 Harvard Kennedy School Cheng Fellow

LinkedIn: Search "Eshwar Bandi Inqui-Lab"

Website: inquilabfoundation.org

3. Product Overview

Inqui-Lab Foundation nurtures problem-solving capacity among first-generation learners from low-income, marginalized communities in India. The organization transforms schools into places of creativity and innovation through design-thinking programs, where students generate solutions to real community problems.

4. Funding Stage

Grant-Funded Nonprofit — Part of the 2023-2024 Launch Lab X GEO cohort. Has partnered with 30+ government schools in India. Recognized by HundrED (global education innovation organization). Registered nonprofit in India.

5. How I Found the Company

I discovered Inqui-Lab Foundation through the Harvard Kennedy School Cheng Fellows program, which selects exceptional students advancing social change. Cross-referencing the founder's HGSE '23 affiliation, I found the organization's focus on design thinking and problem-solving for marginalized Indian students. The tangible student projects — from eco-friendly medicine bags with braille instructions to wheelchair-accessible car seat designs — demonstrate real creative output.

Strengths

  • Focus on underserved population with high potential: First-generation learners in India represent enormous untapped potential. By building problem-solving skills early, Inqui-Lab may produce outsized long-term impact as these students enter the workforce.
  • Tangible, portfolio-ready student outcomes: Student projects (medicine bags, wheelchair car seats) demonstrate that the program produces real innovations, not just abstract "skill development." These outputs make compelling marketing material for funders and partners.

Weaknesses

  • Scale limitations for in-school programs: Working directly with government schools in India requires navigating bureaucracy and teacher training in each new location. This creates inherent scaling challenges compared to digital-first approaches.
  • Nonprofit model limits growth capital: As a registered nonprofit, Inqui-Lab cannot access venture capital. Growth depends on grant funding and donations, which may be slower and less predictable than equity financing.

7. Five Questions for the Team

  1. What is your cost per student served, and how does that compare to other innovation education programs in India?
  2. How do you train teachers in government schools to facilitate design thinking? What does teacher adoption and retention look like?
  3. Have any student projects from Inqui-Lab been commercialized or patented? Is there a pathway from classroom innovation to real-world implementation?
  4. What are your plans for geographic expansion within India — are you focused on specific states, or pursuing national-level partnerships?
  5. Are you considering a hybrid model with digital components that could scale beyond the 30 schools you currently serve?
