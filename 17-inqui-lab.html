I discovered Inqui-Lab Foundation through the Harvard Kennedy School Cheng Fellows program, which selects exceptional students advancing social change. Strengths

  • Focus on underserved population with high potential: First-generation learners in India represent enormous untapped potential. By building problem-solving skills early, Inqui-Lab may produce outsized long-term impact as these students enter the workforce.
  • Tangible, portfolio-ready student outcomes: Student projects (medicine bags, wheelchair car seats) demonstrate that the program produces real innovations, not just abstract "skill development." These outputs make compelling marketing material for funders and partners.

Weaknesses

  • Scale limitations for in-school programs: Working directly with government schools in India requires navigating bureaucracy and teacher training in each new location. This creates inherent scaling challenges compared to digital-first approaches.
  • Nonprofit model limits growth capital: As a registered nonprofit, Inqui-Lab cannot access venture capital. Growth depends on grant funding and donations, which may be slower and less predictable than equity financing.
